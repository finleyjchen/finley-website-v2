Bio

My name is Finley Chen. I am a web developer who makes websites and does other web work for businesses.

I am a person who has been intrigued by flashing lights and screens all my life. In my household, there were screens all around me - my dad's PC, the family iMac, the TV, the cell phones. I remember how excited I was when I received my first Nintendo Gameboy when I was 5 years old. From interest in video games, I started porting Windows games to play them on the family iMac. When I was 11 years old, my grandma traded me her old Acer aspire laptop for my baby blanket (she wanted me to grow out of it). Now, I have accumulated a variety of skills that I'd like to show you.

Me and my dad around 2001.

Learning Linux

The old Acer Aspire laptop my grandma gave me was really old, slow, and clunky, with the bloatware and malware-infected Windows Vista. 11 year old me had to figure out the solutions. After countless hours in internet forums, I stumbled upon Linux forums, leading me to read an article that convinced me that the solution was to install Linux on my laptop. What a start.

At age 11, I had spent a lot of time on Apple computers, but never really understood how a computer worked. The learning process of installing Linux jumpstarted my knowledge by teaching me many important concepts about computers, from the physical parts and how they work together to the kernel and the operating system. Since I was learning in a Linux environment, I learned about networking and penetration testing, which lead me to have interest in Cybersecurity. I am in the process of learning and preparing to get an OSCP certificate.

Learning other programming languages

Have you ever heard of that programming language taught in high school computer science classes that's really hard and frustrating to grasp? Yeah, that's Java. It is an object-oriented programming language that is usually one of the first languages to be taught to students, aside from Python. During my AP Computer Science class in high school and my extended studying and experimenting that sparked from them, I became prominent in Java. It was really appealing to me because Minecraft, one of my favorite games, was built on it. Since almost all video games are created through object-oriented programming, my creativity and interest in video games made Java a great foundational language for me to learn.

Contrary to popular opinion, I really love Java. When there were complex concepts that people seemed to have issues understanding, I understood them. The object-oriented programming language just made sense. My newfound passion for programming led me to learn about Javascript. From Javascript, web programming languages were really easily accessible and I understood them quickly. I have not yet mastered Java or JavaScript, and still have much to learn. I have many projects and concepts that I'd like to finish and showcase here soon. Next, I'd like to get familiar with C, C++, and Objective C.
